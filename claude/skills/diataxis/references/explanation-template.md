# Explanation template

Title pattern: supports an implicit "about" — "About <topic>" / "Why <design choice>" /
"<Topic> explained" / "Understanding <topic>". Bounded by a topic, not a task or a
component.
(Read at leisure, away from the product. The higher, wider view. Opinion is allowed.)

---

# <Title>

<Orienting opening: what topic this discusses and why a reader might care — 2–3
sentences.>

## <Aspect of the topic>

<Discursive prose. Background, context, history, design rationale. "The reason for x is
that…" Connections to related topics, linked liberally — weaving the web of
understanding is the job.>

## <Another aspect — alternatives and trade-offs>

<Discuss what else could have been done and why this approach won. "W is preferred over
z because…" Counter-examples and limitations included — honesty here builds the trust
that reference austerity can't.>

## <Implications for the reader>

<What this understanding changes about how they'll think about the product. Link to the
relevant how-to guides and reference pages — but do not absorb their content.>

---

Checklist before publishing:
- [ ] Bounded by a meaningful topic (title passes the "about" test)
- [ ] No step-by-step instructions and no specification tables (link instead)
- [ ] Makes connections — links to related topics, even beyond immediate scope
- [ ] Discusses alternatives/trade-offs where they exist, with honest opinion
- [ ] Readable start-to-finish away from the product
