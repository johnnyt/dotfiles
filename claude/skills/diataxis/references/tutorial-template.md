# Tutorial template

Title pattern: "Getting started with <thing>" / "Your first <outcome>"
(A lesson, not a task. The reader is learning, and the author owns their success.)

---

# <Title>

In this tutorial, you will <what the learner will have done and learned by the end —
one sentence, concrete>.

## Before you begin

<The complete, explicit list of prerequisites — installed app, account state, device.
Assume nothing. Do not explain why prerequisites are needed.>

## Step 1: <concrete action>

<Imperative instruction. One action or tight action cluster.>

<Narrative of expectations: "You should now see…" — what visible result confirms the
step worked. Every step must have one.>

## Step 2: <concrete action>

<Same shape. No options ("you could also…"), no branches, no rationale. If the urge to
explain arises, write one clause at most and link: "…(see [About <topic>] to learn
why).">

## Step N: <the payoff step>

<The step where the promised outcome becomes visible.>

## What you've done

<Recap in 2–4 sentences: name the things the learner can now do. Link onward: one or
two how-to guides for real tasks, one explanation page for depth.>

---

Checklist before publishing:
- [ ] Single path — zero forks, zero alternatives
- [ ] Every step states its visible result
- [ ] No explanatory paragraphs (links instead)
- [ ] A brand-new user can follow it with only the stated prerequisites
- [ ] Verified end-to-end against the current product build
